Profile

Hi, thanks for coming to visit me at my website. In 1988 two Kiwis went to San Fransisco on a sky diving trip and made me. It was a pretty sweet deal. I got to grow up in a blooming tech capital and enjoy dual NZ and US citizenship. When I was 20 I felt I should stop tinkering with silly robots and undertake a mature topic at university, like Psychology and Criminology. In my final year at Auckland Uni I got a job at Microsoft, primarily I helped people with their small to midsize Microsoft 365 licences. The role pleasently allowed me to talk freely to techies all over New Zealand about their roles and often their dream pet projects, sometimes involving familiar silly robots. A co-worker overheard my chats and donated a Raspberry Pi to me one day. Every night I pushed past my bedtime exploring the file system, and learning what all these cryptic sounding processes were up to. The next logical step was an Arduino, to make my internet things control the real world. I began undertaking the MCSA to learn Windows Server admin and started getting really nutty about tcp/ip, network hardware, antennas, WANs, etc. DevOps let me bring programming back into admin. Powershell was a means to take Windows admin much further than ticking boxes in a GUI management interface. When I transitioned from Hansells to Firmstep I moved from nursing an ongoing Domain Controller in a server room to thinking of server infrastructure as a more ephemeral process of coding and launching scripts-as-infrastructure in AWS. I'm excited for a future in admin that stays fresh by utilizing new practices, where work creates a greater foundation, something at least newer and better than before.
